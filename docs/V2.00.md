### ğŸ’¾ Exporting Edited Photos

When you edit photos in iPhoto, all adjustments are stored **non-destructively** in `.ipo` sidecar files next to your originals. To share or use your edited photos elsewhere, you need to **export** them, which renders the adjustments into new image files.

#### How to Export

**Export Selected Photos:**
1. Select one or more photos in the gallery (edited or unedited)
2. Go to **File** > **Export Selected** in the menu
<img width="1909" height="399" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/a8b10fdf-319c-4590-85f3-3fa4ec45b552" />

3. Choose a destination (or use the default)
4. iPhoto will:
   - Render edited photos with all adjustments applied and save as high-quality JPG (regardless of original format)
   - Copy unedited photos and videos as-is (preserving original format)

**Export All Edited Photos:**
1. Go to **File** > **Export All Edited** in the menu
2. Choose a destination (or use the default)
3. iPhoto will scan your entire library and export all photos that have `.ipo` sidecar files

#### Export Destination Settings

Configure where exported files are saved via **Settings** > **Export Destination**:

- **ğŸ“‚ Basic Library** (default): Exports to an `exported/` folder inside your library root, mirroring your album structure
- **â“ Ask Every Time**: Prompts you to choose a destination folder for each export operation

> ğŸ’¡ **Tip:** Original files remain completely untouched during export. You can safely share exported files while preserving your editable originals in the library.

All photo edits are stored in `.ipo` (iPhoto Output) sidecar files next to your original images:


The `.ipo` file contains:
- Light adjustments (brilliance, exposure, highlights, shadows, etc.)

- Black & white parameters (intensity, neutrals, tone, grain)
- Crop and perspective transforms (vertical/horizontal keystone, straighten angle, crop box)
